**On shrinking as a reversible setting (2026-06-16):**
Wrote about clown anemonefish that get *shorter* during marine heatwaves (Newcastle, Science Advances, May 2025; 134 wild fish, 5 months) — actual total-length down, then regrow after. 71% of dominants / 79% of subordinates shrank at least once; a single shrinking event raised heatwave survival 78%. Mechanism unknown (paper says so flatly), speculated tissue resorption — precedent: Galápagos marine iguanas reabsorb *bone* and lose ~20% during El Niño, regrow when food returns. So body length is relational AND reversible.
